ℹ Deadhead = non-revenue travel between the vendor yard and your first/last stop. The optimizer will flag routes that exceed your scholar ride time limit.

Geographic boundaries (optional)
Set hard limits on how far routes can extend in any direction. The optimizer will exclude any family address outside these boundaries and flag them as unserviceable.
ℹ Boundaries are approximate — the tool uses the geocoded coordinates of the boundary address to set a lat/lon limit. After running the optimizer, any excluded families appear in a warning list so you can follow up with them directly.

Critical communities
Upload priority enrollment zones from your outreach director. The optimizer will anchor a bus stop near each one — even if no family addresses are clustered there yet. These appear as ⭐ stops in the editor and on the map.
What counts as a critical community?
Apartment complexes
High-density housing where families cluster
Community anchors
Boys & Girls Clubs, libraries, community centers
Enrollment hotspots
Neighborhoods where outreach is actively happening

Routing parameters
ℹ Stop locations are placed at the geographic center of each family cluster. For best results, upload your Critical Communities list (Step 3) — those named public locations (Boys & Girls Clubs, libraries, apartment complexes) become the actual stop addresses instead of raw family addresses.
Families beyond this radius are excluded. Rule of thumb: at 12 mph avg speed, 10 miles ≈ 50 min ride, 14 miles ≈ 70 min ride, 18 miles ≈ 90 min ride. Reducing this is the fastest way to bring ride times under 60–90 min.

Reduce max stops — fewer stops = less dwell time (3 min/stop).
